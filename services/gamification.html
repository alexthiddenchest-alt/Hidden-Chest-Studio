Why Gamification

Engagement systems built by people who build games

A lot of gamification work is bolted on after the fact — a points counter added to an app that was never designed around play. Hidden Chest Studio approaches enterprise gamification the other way around: we design the engagement loop first, using real game design mechanics, then build the interface and rewards system around it. That's the process behind LOFINDER, the brand gamification project we built for AirAsia, and it's the same process we bring to every enterprise engagement brief.

Our gamification agency work covers gamified app design, engagement and rewards systems, and enterprise gamification programmes for internal or customer-facing use. Because we're a full game studio first, we're able to bring mechanics that feel genuinely game-like — progression, feedback loops, competition, reward pacing — rather than the shallow badge-and-points layer that a lot of "gamified" products settle for.

This isn't limited to app screens. For Khazanah Nasional, we designed and built a bespoke interactive installation at the historic Carcosa Seri Negara, applying the same engagement-first thinking to a physical, in-venue experience. Whether the deliverable is a mobile app, a web dashboard, or a live installation, the underlying discipline is the same: understand what makes people want to keep going, then build the system around that.

FAQ

Common questions

What does a gamification agency actually do?

A gamification agency applies game design mechanics — points, progress, rewards, competition — to a non-game product or experience, to increase engagement. For AirAsia, that meant designing LOFINDER, a brand gamification project built to engage their audience through play rather than a standard app flow.

What's the difference between gamification and building a full game?

Gamification adds game mechanics to an existing product, app, or campaign — it doesn't require a full game to be built from scratch. If your goal calls for a standalone title instead, that falls under our game development studio work, and we scope each project toward whichever approach fits.

Can you build enterprise gamification systems, not just consumer campaigns?

Yes. Our client work spans consumer brand engagement, like AirAsia's LOFINDER, and enterprise-facing interactive installations, like the bespoke experience we built for Khazanah Nasional at Carcosa Seri Negara. We design engagement systems for both audiences.

Do you build rewards and loyalty mechanics into gamified apps?

Rewards, progress tracking, and loyalty-style mechanics are core to most gamification briefs we take on, and we build them as part of the gamified app itself rather than bolting them on afterward.

How do you measure whether a gamification system is actually working?

We build tracking into the mechanics themselves — completion rates, session frequency, reward redemption, and progression data — so engagement is a measurable number rather than a guess. That data also feeds back into refining the system after launch instead of treating launch as the finish line.
